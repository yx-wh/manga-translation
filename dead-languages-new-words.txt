You have traveled back in time to the year 200 BC with the aim of taking over the world. You brought with you a solar-powered Kindle to which you have downloaded the contents of Wikipedia, Project Gutenberg, and WebMD. You are confident that only you can read its contents, since you alone in the world are fluent in modern English (even the Middle English of Chaucer is centuries away); you know that you can communicate with the locals, since you have prepared for this journey by studying Latin. So far, so good; depending on your strategy for world domination, you can converse with local rulers in the rhetorics of religion, philosophy, governance, and empire; of I am a powerful sorcerer, I have been sent by the gods, I am from the future, I come in peace, and I can help you wage war. The question is what words you use to explain to the locals what the Kindle is.

你带着掌控全世界的远大理想穿越到了公元前200年。你带上了有太阳能电池的Kindle，并把整个维基百科、古登堡计划和网络医生(WebMD)网站下载到里面。你确信只有你能读懂这些内容，因为你是本时空唯一能够熟练读懂现代英语的人（乔叟的中世纪英语还在N个世纪之后）。你为了这次旅行专门学习了拉丁语，因此确信你可以与当地人交谈。目前来看一切都很顺利。根据你制定的掌控世界的战略，你会与当地的统治者谈及宗教话语、哲学、统治术和帝国；你告诉他们“我是一名被上帝派来的强大巫师”，“我来自未来”，“我为和平而来”，以及“我可以帮助你们发动战争”。这里要讨论的是，你要用什么词汇向当地人解释什么是Kindle。


Fortunately, in our own time, lexicographers have been happily working for decades to expand the lexicons of ancient languages with words for modern concepts. Speakers of Yiddish can consult dictionaries that offer words for e-mail, binge watching, and designated driver. Latin aficionados can find a number of glossaries, such as the Vocabula computatralia, that devise new words and phrases for the world of computing. (The Kindle, you might tell your new patrons, is a computatrulum.) By such means, modern languages quietly reshape ancient languages, and living languages dead ones.

幸运的是，在我们所处的时代，词典编撰者在最近数十年间一直在高兴地把那些表达了现代新生事物的词汇添加到古代语言中。说意第绪语的人可以查到记录有“电子邮件”“酒后代驾司机”“一口气看完”的辞典。拉丁语爱好者会发现有许多词库——例如Vocabula computatralia——为计算机世界发明了新的词汇和短语（你可以告诉你新时空的庇护人（靠山？），Kindle是一个computatrulum）。现代语言/活语言就这样悄悄地改变了古代语言/死语言。

 

隐喻拓展

The interesting part of this alchemy is when new terms in the ancient language preserve figures of speech or metaphorical extensions that originated in the modern one. (Metaphorical extension is a kind of semantic change in which a word broadens its meaning to apply to a new domain. For example, surfing used to be something you do in California with a surfboard; now it’s something you do in California with a keyboard.) As a Latin term for a computer window, the Vocabula proposes fenestra—literally, window. Ancient Romans would likely find the usage puzzling, though not wholly unintelligible. (As the historian Anne Friedberg notes, the ubiquity of the window as a functional metaphor for pictorial space goes back to Leon Battista Alberti in the 15th century. However, classical artists sometimes used trompe l’oeil effects, which can entail ‘looking through’ the surface of an image.) The cursor pad, however—muris tabula, or mouse tablet, according to the rules of the glossary—would probably create the wrong impression.

这种方法的有趣之处在于，古语言中的新词保留有源于当代语言的修辞手法或隐喻拓展。（隐喻拓展是一种语义变化，在这种变化中，词语的意思拓展到一个新的领域。例如，在加利福尼亚用冲浪板进行一种运动可以称作surfing，但现在，在加利福尼亚用键盘进行的一种活动，也可以称作surfing。）Vocabula建议，用fenestra这个词表示计算机中的“窗口”——这个词的字面意义就是窗户。古罗马人可能会对这个用法感到奇怪，但至少不是完全不能理解。（历史学家Anne Friedberg注意到，window一词能够用来普遍表示绘图区域，能追溯到15世纪的Leon Battista Alberti。然而，有时古典艺术家会利用视觉陷阱，这种效应“看穿”了图像表面。）触摸板——根据单词表的规定应该是muris tabula，直译成英语是mouse tablet——这个词可能会给古人产生错误的印象。

 

拉丁语中的spam、computer和keyboard

Forget about explaining spam; the English verb for sending junk messages online, which derives from the toxic combination of Monty Python and first-generation trolls, Latinizes as saginare, that is, to make fat. The Latin word for keyboard—claviatura, or instrument for the act of keying—relies on a sense of the word clavis, or key, that would not emerge until the proliferation of musical instruments with keyboards in the14th century. Even the English word computer—which derives from the Latin computare, or to count—took a wayward path through metaphor to reach its present meaning. In the early 20th century, computer was a job title referring to someone who did mathematical reckoning for a living. (In fact, it was a job that, like secretary, tended to belong to women.) Digital computers received their name because they could perform the work of human computers. The antique equivalent might be servus, servant.

放弃和古人解释spam吧；这是个英语中用于指在线发送垃圾信息的动词，它衍生自Monty Python（蒙提派森）和第一代网络喷子的奇葩组合，其拉丁化拼写为saginare，亦即使……变胖的意思。拉丁语中的keyboard是claviatura，即用于按键的工具，这个词衍生自clavis，但它有了“键”这个义项，是在14世纪键盘乐器大普及之后。甚至连英语中的computer——这个源自拉丁语computare的词——也是通过隐喻才演变成了如今的意思。在20世纪早期，computer指的是那些以替人算东西为生的人（其实，在那时候，这个职业和秘书一样，倾向于由女性担任）。电子计算机正是因为能完成人类计算员的工作才也被称作computer的。古语言中的对应词可以是servus，亦即英语的servant。

 

隐喻的作用（和死亡）

What do these back-formations on the level of metaphor tell us about the interaction of language and culture? In the case of this specific example, they remind us of the active role that metaphor has played in shaping computing technologies and making them comprehensible to users. “[W]hereas other technologies may be said to have a nature of their own and thus to exercise some agency in their design,” writes the historian Michael Mahoney, “the computer has no such nature. Or, rather, its nature is protean; the computer is—or certainly was at the beginning—what we make of it (or now have made of it) through the tasks we set for it and the programs we write for it.” Metaphors helped us to make and share blueprints for what we wanted to do with computing technologies and then market those technologies to a skittish public.

这些隐喻层面上的逆构法说明了哪些语言和文化之间的互动呢？在这个特定的例子中，它告诉了我们，在塑造并向用户普及计算机技术的过程中，隐喻起到了重要作用。历史学家Michael Mahoney写道：“其他技术可能会被认为具有其天生的本质特点，这些本质特点会在在设计中起到了一定的作用。而计算机并没有这种本质特点，或者说，计算机的本质特点就是“变化”。在完成我们设定的任务以及为计算机编写的程序中，我们用到了什么，计算机就是——或者至少可以说在刚开始的时候是——什么。”隐喻帮助我们说明自己/理解别人想利用计算技术干些什么，帮助我们将这些技术商业推广到对其感到不安的大众。

 

On the level of language, this phenomenon highlights the profound place of metaphor in all language production. As the OxfordWords blog has previously discussed, ordinary languageuse abounds with buried metaphors, even when we are not explicitly using figurative language. We reserve the term dead language for tongues that no longer have native speakers, but even living languages take many of their words and expressions from metaphors that are dead in the sense that they refer to ideas or ways of life that have no living adherents. New metaphors emerge, grow, and die too quickly for us to sweep away the old husks; we always speak in a conspiracy of the present and the past, although the juxtaposition is more noticeable in the case of Latin. Living languages reshape dead ones for the same reasons, and in the same ways, that living languages reshape themselves.

在语言层面上，这一现象展现了隐喻在语言生成中的基础地位。在牛津辞典博客中曾经讨论过，就算我们不会明确使用比喻性的说法，隐喻仍在日常语言中随处可见。我们将“死语言”这一名称留给了那些不再有母语者的语言，但即使是在活语言中，仍然有很多词语和表达中出现了“死”隐喻。说它们是“死”隐喻，是因为它们指向的想法或者生活方式，在现代已经没有继承者了。新的隐喻出现、成长然后消失，这个过程快到我们都来不及剥去旧隐喻的外衣。我们总是在过去和现在的合力作用下中使用语言，尽管这种对立在拉丁语更加明显。活语言因同样的原因重塑死语言，而活语言也以用样的方式重塑它们自己。

 

让过去的成为现代的

Ultimately, this phenomenon may help to remind us of the difference between making the traditional modern and really modernizing tradition. The classical distinction between de facto and de jure, for instance, may need augmentation in an era in which software code regulates our activity as a power separate from convention or the courts, as Lawrence Lessigh as summarized with the phrase ‘code is law’. (De facto, de jure, de codex.) Adding our own words—or words that embed our own mental models—to the lexicon of antiquity is fun. Figuring out how the mental models of the past can adjust to the present day is a more challenging task, as we see, for example, in the case of the U.S. Constitution. Though everything changes, nothing perishes, even on the World Wide Web—or rather, the Tela Totius Terrae.

最终，这种现象可能有助于提醒我们，让传统变得现代和真正现代化的传统之间的区别。比如，事实上（de facto）和法理上（de jure）之间的传统区别，在这个时代也许需要修订。软件代码作为惯例或法庭之外的力量，调节了我们的活动，这就如Lawrence Lessig曾总结的一句话“代码即法律”(De facto, de jure, de codex.)。把我们这个时代的词语——或者把嵌入我们思维模式的词语——加进古典词汇是件趣事。搞清过去的思维模式如何应用于当代，是一个更具挑战性的任务，例如正如我们所见的美国宪法的例子。诸事皆变，却无消亡，即使在互联网（或用拉丁语说是Tela Totius Terrae）上亦是如此。[似乎为拉丁谚语。]
